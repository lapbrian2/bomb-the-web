The Elephant Incident

My grandpa tagged an actual elephant at the Philadelphia Zoo in '67. The elephant reportedly loved it. The zookeeper did not. Grandpa says the elephant trumpeted in approval. The judge was less enthusiastic. Family tradition now: we only tag animals that weigh over 4 tons. It's a very specific rule.

404 CREW · Internet

I Hate You: A Love Story

Sprayed "I HATE YOU" on a highway overpass. Drove 2 miles. Got inspired again. Sprayed "I STILL HATE YOU." Someone added "WHO HURT YOU?" underneath. Then someone else wrote "KAREN." Became the most visited overpass in New Jersey. They made it a historical landmark. I'm technically a civil engineer now.

Visible From Space

Painted a piece on the LA river bank so large it showed up on Google Earth. My mom found it while looking for directions to Target. Called me screaming. Not because I'm a vandal. Because the letter spacing was off. "Your S is too close to the A!" Mom, it's 250 feet wide. "EXACTLY. No excuses at that scale."

1000 Panels, 1000 Days

Set out to paint 1000 train panels in 1000 days. Day 847: realized I'd been painting the same train. It's a short loop. The conductor started waving at me. By day 900 he was bringing me coffee. Day 1000: he asked me to be his best man. I said yes. The wedding invitations were hand-buffed.

The Poetic Throne

Started writing poetry in bathroom stalls. First piece: "Here I sit on the pooper, giving birth to a state trooper." Won a Pushcart Prize nomination. Just kidding. But the janitor at Denny's started a whole literary magazine from stall #3. It's called "Flush Fiction." Six issues strong. Only available in restrooms. Five stars on Yelp.

The Bot That Bombed

Trained an AI to generate graffiti tags. First output: "HELLO WORLD" in Comic Sans. Threw the laptop off a bridge. Fished it out. Tried again. It generated "PLEASE DO NOT VANDALIZE." I have created the world's first snitch algorithm. The cops love it. My crew disowned me. The AI now works in code review.

The Crew Rules

01
Never tag a bathroom stall below eye level. That's disrespectful to the person sitting there. They're going through enough.
02
If your tag takes longer than 47 seconds, it's a mural. Murals require a permit, a manifesto, and at least one argument with your crew about color theory.
03
Comic Sans is grounds for immediate crew expulsion. No trial. No appeal. Your cans will be confiscated and given to a kindergarten.
04
Always carry a decoy sketchbook. If confronted, open it to a page of flowers. "Officer, I'm a botanical illustrator." Works 60% of the time, every time.
05
Never go over someone else's piece. Unless it's in Comic Sans. Then it's community service.
06
Your tag name cannot be your real name. We had a guy named "Steve" who tagged "STEVE" everywhere. He got caught immediately. We don't talk about Steve.
07
If your mom compliments your graffiti, start over. You've gone too mainstream. Time to reinvent your entire style, identity, and possibly your name.
08
The cap stays on the can until the lookout says "clear." We lost three good writers to premature spraying. RIP their sneakers.
09
A piece isn't finished until you've stepped back and said "yeah." If you said "hmm" instead, add more drips. Drips fix everything. Drips are the CSS of graffiti.
10
This website counts as a piece. If you're reading this, you've been tagged. Welcome to the crew. Your initiation is opening DevTools and typing the Konami code.
